An INTEgrated stReet tree VALuation-based solution for minimizing the uneven street tree distribution in Cities

Project concept:

INTERVAL is an INTEgrated stReet tree VALuation based solution for minimising the uneven street tree distribution in cities. This research aims to develop a decision support system to increase the street tree density in Dublin City to promote climate neutrality and minimise environmental injustice that originates due to the inequality of street tree distribution.

Engagement of local communities and citizens for knowledge co-creation

Advanced geospatial technologies to conduct a tree density survey

Ecosystem services database with the most common street tree species in Dublin

Prioritised Areas Identification that need additional street trees

Project Team

Decision Support Platform Co-design

INTERVAL: Minimise environmental injustice by addressing the unequal distribution of urban street trees through community-driven scientific research and assessing the value of ecosystem services.
