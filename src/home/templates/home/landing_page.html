INTERVAL Project

The INTERVAL Project is dedicated to enhancing urban forestry and environmental sustainability by leveraging advanced technologies like AI and remote sensing while fostering community engagement. It addresses key challenges such as uneven tree distribution, insufficient knowledge, and poor management through the INTERVAL Tree Information System Platform. This innovative platform integrates real-time data, citizen science, and collaboration with local communities and authorities to drive better decision-making, raise awareness, and promote shared responsibility for green spaces. By bridging gaps in data and accessibility, INTERVAL aims to create healthier, more sustainable urban environments and inspire collective action for a greener future.

Key Challenges

  • Lack of Tree Information: There is insufficient data or accessible knowledge about existing trees, their health, and locations, which limits effective planning and decision-making.

  • Uneven Tree Distribution:Trees are not evenly distributed across urban areas, leading to disparities in environmental benefits and aesthetics.

  • Improper Tree Management:Maintenance and care of trees are inadequate, resulting in poor health and reduced effectiveness in providing environmental benefits.

  • Lack of Communication: Collaboration and information-sharing among stakeholders, such as local communities, organizations, and authorities, are limited, hindering coordinated efforts for tree conservation.

  • Lack of Knowledge on Tree Benefits:Awareness of the ecological, social, and economic benefits provided by trees is low, reducing motivation for conservation and planting efforts.

  • Lack of Accessibility: Physical or systemic barriers prevent people from interacting with trees and green spaces, reducing community engagement with nature.

Solution Framework

  • Remote Sensing & Earth Observation: Leveraging advanced technologies such as satellite imagery and drones to collect data on tree health, distribution, and environmental impact.

  • Google Street View and Mapillary Data: Utilizing visual data from platforms like Google Street View and Mapillary for mapping and monitoring urban trees and vegetation.

  • Citizen Science Apps and Platforms: Engaging the public through mobile applications and online platforms to crowdsource data collection and encourage community participation in environmental conservation efforts.

  • Local Communities: Involving community members to raise awareness, participate in conservation efforts, and provide localized knowledge for better decision-making.

  • Relevant Authorities:Collaborating with governmental and non-governmental organizations responsible for urban planning, forestry management, and environmental policies.

  • Societal Champions: Partnering with individuals or groups passionate about environmental advocacy to lead initiatives and inspire broader community engagement.
