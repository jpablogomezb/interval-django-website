INTERVAL Project Crumlin Solutions World Café

held on November 2nd, 2024

Workshop Summary

The INTERVAL Project hosted the Crumlin Solutions World Café in November to foster collaboration between community members and key stakeholders, including Dublin City Council. The workshop leveraged the World Café Method, which encourages dynamic, rotating group discussions to identify gaps, brainstorm solutions, and devise implementation strategies for enhancing Crumlin's green infrastructure.

Purpose of the Workshop

The primary goal was to present tree data collected from the Crumlin area back to the community, providing a platform to:

  • Highlight gaps in existing tree coverage.
  • Explore viable solutions and their constraints.
  • Develop actionable strategies for improving the local green infrastructure.

Workshop Activities

  1. Data Presentation: The collected tree data was visually shared through maps, highlighting spatial gaps in tree distribution.
  2. World Café Sessions: Participants worked collaboratively in rotating groups to:
    • Identify key areas needing green development.
    • Brainstorm practical solutions and constraints.
    • Propose actionable strategies for implementing solutions.
  3. Panel Q&A: Experts and community leaders engaged in a discussion to address questions and enhance knowledge transfer between stakeholders.

Key Achievements

  • Identification of priority areas for green development in Crumlin.
  • Creation of tailored solutions for these areas, accounting for local constraints.
  • Development of an implementation strategy to address community needs effectively.
  • Strengthening communication channels between Crumlin residents and Dublin City Council, fostering trust and collaboration for future projects.

Community Tree Engagement Activities

held on July 13, 2024

Workshop Summary

The INTERVAL Project organized an impactful Community Tree Engagement Event in Crumlin, Dublin. This initiative brought together local residents, environmental enthusiasts, and stakeholders to collaboratively learn and contribute to a greener urban environment.

Purpose of the Workshop

The event aimed to empower the community with the knowledge and tools needed to actively participate in urban greening efforts. By fostering a sense of ownership and responsibility, we sought to bridge the gap between citizens and sustainable urban development:

Workshop Activities

  1. Training Sessions:
    • Attendees were trained on using an open-source platform designed specifically for mapping and managing trees.
    • Hands-on demonstrations were conducted to teach participants how to map trees effectively in parks and along streets.
  2. Tree Mapping in Action:
    • Residents worked together to map existing trees in Crumlin, identifying species, sizes, and locations.
    • This effort contributed to the creation of a more comprehensive tree inventory for the area.
  3. Discussions on Urban Greening:
    • Participants engaged in group discussions to share insights on the importance of tree canopy coverage.
    • Ideas were shared on how individuals and communities could support ongoing and future greening initiatives.

Outcomes

  • Community Empowerment: Local residents gained valuable skills and knowledge about urban forestry and its significance for environmental well-being.
  • Data Collection: A significant number of trees were mapped during the event, providing critical data to enhance Crumlin’s tree inventory.
  • Stronger Connections: The event strengthened relationships between the local community and the project team, fostering collaboration for future initiatives.
